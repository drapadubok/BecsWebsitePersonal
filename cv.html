    My main competencies
    Data analysis
    After several years of studying how brains of interacting individuals work by analyzing fMRI data with statistical modeling and machine learning, I learned how to collect, manipulate and inspect large data sets (~100gb – 1tb scale), process them with highly paralellized pipelines and analyze them to derive relevant insights. I can use these skills to extract and clean data, analyse patterns to reveal what do people like and dislike from the data that comes with their activity or predict how a trend may develop.
    Data mining and visualization infrastructure
    I've built several analytic pipelines, which include data storage, processing, analysis and visualizations. One of my main principles is ability of third person to understand both the code and the mechanism of pipeline, so clean and readable code, as well as intuitive explanation – are my priorities. I used cluster computing potential that was present in Aalto University to create multiple analytic utilities that took advantage of parallelization, saving massive amount of laboratory time by running the analysis that would normally take several days in half an hour. I strive to make the tools reusable by other people, including ones without solid technical background.
    Software development
    My tools for running pattern classification algorithms, general linear models and fMRI data preprocessing were used by both my laboratory and collaborators. I use Python, Matlab and R for predictive modeling, machine learning and data mining and cleaning, and use Git as version control system. I have experience with both SQL and NoSQL databases and can build simple web applications using Flask. I am an experienced Linux user and feel most comfortable in Linux and Mac OS environments.
    Project management
    My work as a PhD candidate allowed me to design, plan and carry out multiple research projects, involving experiment planning, data collection, analysis, writing and reporting. I was actively participating in filling the laboratory wiki, providing utilities to collaborators and introducing new people to our tool-kits and practices. I successfully met deadlines and was able to keep up with project schedule even when faced with multiple complications.
    Communications
    Every project I was working on was concluded by scientific research article. After writing several research papers now published in high-profile journals, I acquired good written communication skills. I presented my research at multiple international conferences in talks and poster sessions. I'm confident and comfortable at speaking in front of people, and enjoy using intuitive explanations and metaphors to help listeners' understanding of the topic.
    Teamwork
    My projects involved collaboration with psychologists, statisticians, physicians and neuroscientists, which allowed me to enhance my skills in making very different kind of specialists work together efficiently. I'm tactful and persuasive communicator and leader with strong feeling of personal responsibility. I thrive in environment where a lot of collaboration with heterogenous specialists is possible. In my work with colleagues I am able to take responsibility and lead the way, but also am keen and ready to find compromises and believe that mistakes are one of the best ways to learn. I communicate well both with very technically minded and also individuals with more abstract, artistic and humanitarian backgrounds. Finally, I like to be surrounded by smarter people.
    Teaching
    I assisted in teaching several courses at university, and am happy to share my knowledge and skills. I'm patient and attentive, am capable of transforming complex concepts into intuitive examples and explanations. I enjoy public speaking and telling stories to highlight teaching material.
